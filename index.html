LASA Expo

A student-led technology exposition at our school

February 20th, 2024 during lunch in Mr. Shockey’s Room

Featured Projects

Virtual Reality Demo

Come and experience a demo of the latest technology in virtual reality. Try on a VR headset that was acquired by the CS team for an independent study project.

Panel by Obl.ong

An application for multi-tenant domain services (registry, registrar, zone management, and more) built with Ruby on Rails. (This site uses an obl.ong domain!)

RachitCaster

A maze game built using a custom raytracing engine. Explore the fascinating possibilities of graphics programming.

Hob Physics Engine

A 2D physics engine supporting collisions and gravitational forces. It can handle tens of thousands of particles, and it can be used to simulate various astrophysical events such as the formation of a galaxy.

Flight Simulation

Did you know a hobbyist can simulate flying an aircraft realistically from their own home? Explore the world of flight simulation with a demo.

Arduino & Raspberry Pi

Microprocessors and whole computers are now readily available to us inexpensively. Combining them with physical equipment such as LED strips can produce fascinating results.

Creating a Python Library

Learn how to publish your code to be usable by other people, and find out more about making your project well-documented and accessible.

Facial Emotion Detection AI

It is possible to do interesting things with AI that seemed way more difficult before. Join us to see how AI is used for image classification tasks.

FangEngine

Play against a chess computer built by a LASA student. Can you beat it? Or better yet, can you code it to become unbeatable?

Gas Flare Monitors

Computer science has many real-world impacts, some of which may not be immediately obvious. See how software can be used to detect unlit gas flares to improve safety at powerplants.
